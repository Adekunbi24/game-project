Grade 4 - Adjectives

Welcome!

Before we dive in you should understand what Adjectives are!

Adjectives are one of the most exciting part of speech we have.
It allows us to describe to our friends our favourite movie, food and which we disliked

Adjectives are defined as a part of speech used to describe and provide more information
about a noun or a pronoun. Adjectives are found after the verb or before the noun or pronoun it describes.

Adjectives describe for example; color, size, age, shape, origin, distance, time, temperature.

Forms of Adjectives

Adjectives are used to compare similar qualities of different subjects that perform the same action.

(1) Absolute Adjective: This form of adjective is used in its original form.
For example, "This food is delicious". There is no other subject to be compared that is why this form is used.
(2) The form is used when two subjects performing the same action or having the same quality is compared.
For Example, "My television is bigger than my computer"
" The book I read yesterday was more interesting than the one I read today"
(3) The form of adjective is used when comparing the same quality of two or more subjects
and to describe that a subject is superior to two or more subjects in performing an action. For example, " My dad is the smartest Doctor".
"This drama novel is the most interesting book I have ever read."

There are different types of adjectives in the English language:

(1) Numeric: six, one hundred and one etc.

(2)Quantitative: more, all, some, half, more than enough etc.

(3)Qualitative: colour, size, smell etc.

(4)Possessive: my, his, their, your etc.

(5)Interrogative: which, whose, what etc.

(6)Demonstrative: this, that, those, these etc.
